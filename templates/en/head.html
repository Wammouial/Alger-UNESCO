Algiers

Did you know? It’s Algiers that gave its name to the country and not the other way.

Did you know? Until the 10th century, Algiers was called “Icosium”.

Did you know? Napoleon III used to live in one of Algiers’ palaces in the Kasbah.

Did you know? In 1716 an earthquake nearly destroyed Algiers. Magnitude 7.5 on the Richter's magnitude scale.

Did you know? It’s in the 10th century that Algiers was founded, under the noun of “El-Djazaïr”.

Did you know? Algiers used to be the capital of ‘free France’ between 1942 and 1944.

Did you know? Algiers is the first Maghreb agglomeration (approximately 8 million inhabitants).

Did you know? From the 16th century till the 19th century, the pirate base of Algiers resisted the British navy.
